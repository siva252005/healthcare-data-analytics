📊 VISUALIZATION SUMMARY – HEALTHCARE ANALYSIS

1. Age vs Charges (Line Plot):
Shows that healthcare costs increase with age.
Older individuals tend to have higher medical expenses.

2. BMI vs Charges (Scatter Plot):
Indicates a positive relationship between BMI and charges.
Higher BMI (especially >30) leads to increased costs.

3. Smoker vs Charges (Box Plot):
Clearly shows smokers have significantly higher charges.
Smoking is the strongest cost-driving factor.

4. Region vs Charges (Bar Chart):
Highlights variation in healthcare costs across regions.
Southeast region has the highest average charges.

5. Children vs Charges (Box Plot):
Shows slight increase in cost with more children.
However, impact is less compared to smoking and BMI.

6. Risk Category Distribution (Bar Chart):
Majority of patients fall under Medium Risk.
High Risk group is smaller but contributes heavily to cost.

7. Charges Distribution (Histogram):
Shows right-skewed distribution.
Few patients incur very high costs (outliers).
