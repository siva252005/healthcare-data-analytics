📊 BUSINESS INSIGHTS – HEALTHCARE ANALYSIS

1. Smoking is the strongest cost driver:
Smokers incur nearly 3x higher medical expenses compared to non-smokers.

2. BMI significantly impacts healthcare cost:
Patients with BMI > 30 (Obese) tend to have higher insurance charges.

3. Medium Risk group dominates:
Majority of patients fall under Medium Risk due to either smoking or high BMI.

4. High Risk group is small but critical:
A small percentage of patients (smoker + high BMI + age > 50) contribute heavily to total cost.

5. Regional variation exists:
Southeast region shows the highest healthcare costs, indicating possible lifestyle or environmental factors.

6. Healthcare cost increases with age:
Older individuals contribute more to cumulative medical expenses.

7. Cost distribution is skewed:
A small group of patients contributes to a large portion of total healthcare spending.
